Lifted product code

Name
Lifted product code[1] 
Description
A family of \([[n,k,d]]\) CSS codes whose construction is based on two linear seed codes \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) with a free action of a group G.
Protection
The lifted product codes contain [2] families of QLDPC codes with the parameters \([[n, k = \Theta(n), d = \Theta(n)]]\).
Decoder

BP-OSD [1].

Notes

Lifted product codes can also be defined over arbitrary matrix rings.

Parent(s)
  • Calderbank-Shor-Steane (CSS) stabilizer code (Since lifted product codes are defined by a length-three chain complex they are CSS codes.)
  • Homological product code (Lifted product obtained from the homological product over a group algebra \(\F_2 G\) of two length-two chain complexes over \(\F_2 G\), i.e., classical binary codes with a free action of the group \(G\).)
  • Quantum low-density parity-check (QLDPC) code (Sparsity of seed codes \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) preserved by the lifted product (up to a constant factor), yielding a QLDPC code.)
Cousin(s)
  • Hypergraph product code (Lifted-product codes for trivial group G are hypergraph-product codes.)
