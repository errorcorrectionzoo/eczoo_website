Gold code[1]

Description

A \([2^r-1, 2r ]\) is a cyclic linear code characterized by the generator polynomial of degree \(r\) of two maximum-period sequences of period \(2^r-1\) with absolute cross-correlation \( \leq 2^((r+2)/2)\). Gold codewords are generated using \(m\)-sequences \(x\) and \(y\), which are codewords of simplex codes with check polynomials of degree \(r\) [1].

Encoding

Information bits are initialized in the shift registers of the two \(m\)-sequences \(x\) and \(y\).

Decoding

General decoding is done by building a sparse parity check matrix, followed by applying an iterative message passing alogirithm. [2].

Realizations

Used in for synchronization purposes in telecommunication [3] and satelite navigation \( (\url{https://www.kowoma-gps.de/signals/})\).GPS C/A [4].
