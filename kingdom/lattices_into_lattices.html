Lattice Kingdom

Welcome to the Lattice Kingdom.

Lattice-based code Encodes states (codewords) in coordinates of a lattice in the \(n\)-dimensional real coordinate space \(\mathbb{R}^n\). The number of codewords may be infinite because the coordinate space is infinite-dimensional, so various restricted versions have to be constructed in practice. Since lattices are closed under addition, lattice-based codes can be thought of as linear codes over the reals. Parents: Error-correcting code. Cousins: Linear binary code.
